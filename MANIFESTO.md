# Sovereign Accountable Commons: Manifesto

## The Problem

Professional services - law, accountancy, medicine, financial advice - are regulated.
Regulators set minimum standards of conduct. Practitioners who fall below those standards
face sanctions.

This is not the same as accountability.

Regulation asks: did you follow the rules?
Accountability asks: did the person you served get what they were entitled to expect?

These are different questions. Regulation can be satisfied while the client is failed.
A solicitor can comply with all SRA rules while still running a matter in a way that
costs the client twice what it should have cost and produces half the outcome they needed.

The gap between regulation and accountability is where most professional service failures
live. Not in gross misconduct. In the ordinary operation of a system that has not been
designed with the client's outcome at its centre.

## The Claim

We claim that accountability is possible. That professional services can be structured
so that the incentives of the practitioner and the interests of the client point in the
same direction. That this is not utopian but practical.

We point to Meridian Law LLP, which adopted FairShares governance in 2022 and removed
the billing target culture that had made accountability structurally impossible at their
previous firm. We point to Halcyon Chambers, which joined SAC in 2024 not because it was
required to but because it believed in being held to account for outcomes, not just conduct.

These are not perfect organisations. They are accountable ones. The distinction matters.

## What We Do

SAC maintains a membership framework with three components:

**Commitment.** Members commit to specific, verifiable standards: transparent fee structures,
documented reasoning, outcome reporting, client feedback processes.

**Audit.** SAC audits member commitments annually. Not a tick-box audit. A real assessment
of whether the member is doing what they said they would do.

**Accountability.** If a member is not meeting their commitments, SAC says so. Publicly.
Members who consistently fail are removed from the SAC register.

## What We Do Not Do

We do not provide legal services. We do not regulate. We do not replace the SRA, the BSB,
or any other regulator. We are not a complaints body.

We hold members accountable to the standards they chose to adopt. Nothing more.

## Who We Are For

Anyone who has needed a professional service and not known whether the person serving them
was working for them or for their own billing target.

Anyone who has received an invoice they could not understand and had no way to challenge.

Anyone who has been told "that is how it works" when what they meant was "that is how we
have arranged for it to work, to our benefit."

We are for them.
