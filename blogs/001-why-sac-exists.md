# Why the Sovereign Accountable Commons Exists

*Rhys Avery, Coordinator, January 2024*

I wrote a paper in late 2022 called "The Accountability Gap." It was about professional services.

The gap I identified was this: regulation measures conduct. Accountability measures outcome. These are not the same thing. A professional can comply perfectly with every regulatory rule while still failing the person they were supposed to serve.

This is not a cynical observation. Regulators are not designed to measure outcomes. They are designed to set minimum standards of conduct and sanction those who fall below them. That is a useful function. It is not sufficient.

The question I kept being asked, when I described the gap, was: what would sufficient look like?

This is what SAC is. An attempt to answer that question.

We do not replace regulators. We do not provide legal services. We hold members accountable to the standards they chose when they joined. If they meet those standards, they stay on the register. If they do not, they are removed.

It is a simple mechanism. The difficulty is in the auditing: actually checking whether members are doing what they said they would do. That is where most accountability frameworks fail. They require self-reporting. We do not.

We audit. We ask for evidence. We talk to clients. We make a judgment.

That is harder. It is also the only way it works.
