# Halcyon Chambers and Meridian Law Join SAC

*Isla Moss, Community Liaison, June 2024*

In March 2024, Halcyon Chambers became the first institutional member of the Sovereign Accountable Commons. In June, Meridian Law LLP joined.

I want to say something about what this meant to me, and then something about what it means more broadly.

What it meant to me: I joined this project because I kept meeting people who had been failed by professional services and had no way to say so that anyone would listen to. The regulator route is long, adversarial, and rarely produces the outcome the person needed. The complaints process at most firms is managed by the firm.

When Halcyon Chambers joined SAC, they signed a commitment that said, in substance: if our clients say we failed them, we will sit down with SAC and show our working. We will not manage the complaint. We will answer for it.

That is not a small thing for a barristers chambers to commit to.

What it means more broadly: these are the first two. We need more. Not because two members is a failure, but because the framework is only useful at scale. The more professional services organisations that join SAC, the more the accountability norm becomes the expected one, not the exceptional one.

If you work in professional services and you believe the accountability gap is real: join us.

If you have been through a professional service failure and you want to be part of building something that prevents it from happening to someone else: join us.

The door is open.
