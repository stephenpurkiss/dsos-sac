# Founding of the Sovereign Accountable Commons

## The Conversation

In late 2022, Rhys Avery was working as a community development officer at a housing charity
in Cardiff. He had spent three years helping residents navigate housing disputes, benefits
appeals, and occasionally estate matters when a family member died without a will.

He noticed something. The people he was helping were not served badly because their advisers
were incompetent or malicious. They were served badly because the system those advisers
operated in was not designed to serve them. The incentives pointed elsewhere.

He wrote a paper. He called it "The Accountability Gap." He sent it to twelve people.
Three responded. Fatima Hakeem was one of them.

Fatima was a legal aid caseworker in Manchester. She had the same observation from a
different vantage point: the gap between what the client was promised and what they received
was not bridged by regulation, because regulation measured conduct, not outcome.

Kofi Mensah was working in governance consultancy. He had spent years designing accountability
frameworks for public sector organisations. He read Rhys's paper and saw the private
sector application immediately.

Isla Moss was a community organiser in Birmingham. She brought the question Rhys, Fatima,
and Kofi had not fully answered: how do you build something that works for the person with
the least power in the room?

## 2023

The four of them spent 2023 designing the framework. They consulted practitioners,
regulators, academics, and most importantly, individuals who had been through contested
professional service experiences.

The framework they built had three components: commitment, audit, accountability.
It was not new. It was the application of existing accountability principles to a
sector where they had not been applied consistently.

## 2024

SAC incorporated as a community interest company in January 2024.
Halcyon Chambers became the first institutional member in March 2024.
Meridian Law LLP joined in June 2024.

Stephen Purkiss became an individual member in December 2024, following a referral from
a community network in Manchester. His case - executor removal and solicitor costs dispute -
was the first matter in which SAC referred a client to both an institutional member
solicitor (Meridian Law) and an institutional member chambers (Halcyon Chambers).

## The Name

Sovereign: the individual is sovereign over their own data, their own narrative,
their own experience. Accountability flows to them, not from them.

Accountable: not compliant. Not regulated. Accountable.

Commons: the framework is held collectively. No single member owns it.
It belongs to everyone who has committed to it.
