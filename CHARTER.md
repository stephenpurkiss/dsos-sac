# Sovereign Accountable Commons: Charter

Version 1.0, January 2024. Adopted by founding members March 2024.

## Article 1: Nature of the SAC

The Sovereign Accountable Commons is a governance methodology and membership framework.
It is not a service provider, a regulator, or a legal entity acting on behalf of its members.

SAC exists to define and maintain the accountability standards that member organisations
adopt voluntarily and are audited against annually.

The rules of SAC are set by its members collectively. No single member, including
founding members, has a unilateral right to change the charter. Amendments require
a supermajority of institutional members and must be ratified by the community assembly.

## Article 2: Membership Categories

**Individual members**
Any person may apply for individual membership. There is no fee. Individual members
commit to accurate self-reporting of their experience with professional services and
to participating in the feedback processes of member organisations.

**Practitioner members**
Individuals providing professional services who commit to the practitioner standards
in Annex A. Subject to peer review on application and annually thereafter.

**Institutional members**
Organisations that have passed the SAC accountability audit and commit to annual
reporting and independent audit. Institutional members are listed on the SAC register
and may display the SAC mark.

## Article 3: Institutional Member Commitments

Institutional members commit to all of the following:

1. **Transparent fee structures.** Fees are disclosed in advance. No surprise invoices.
   Material changes to fee estimates are communicated before they are incurred.

2. **Documented reasoning.** Advice is given in writing. The basis for recommendations
   is explained, not asserted. Where advice is given verbally, written confirmation
   follows within 24 hours.

3. **Outcome reporting.** Members report outcomes (not just process) to SAC annually.
   Outcome is defined as: did the client achieve what they needed? Not: was the process
   compliant?

4. **Client feedback processes.** Members have an active process for collecting feedback.
   Feedback is reviewed by senior leadership, not just by the team that delivered the work.

5. **Escalation independence.** Clients have a named contact independent of the lead
   fee earner for escalation of concerns. This contact has authority to act.

6. **Data sovereignty.** Client data belongs to the client. Members provide full data
   export on request within 5 working days, at no charge.

## Article 4: The Accountability Audit

SAC conducts annual audits of institutional members. Audits are conducted by a panel
that includes at least one member from outside the sector being audited.

Audits assess:
- Evidence of fee transparency (sample billing reviewed)
- Evidence of documented reasoning (sample files reviewed with consent)
- Outcome data reported by the member
- Client feedback processes (policy and evidence of operation)
- Escalation contact (named, contactable, independently briefed)
- Data export capability (tested)

Audit reports are shared with the member before publication. Members may dispute
findings within 30 days. Disputes are adjudicated by the community assembly.

## Article 5: Publication

All institutional member audit reports are published on the SAC register within 60 days
of the audit. Summary ratings (meeting commitments / partially meeting / not meeting)
are displayed on the public register.

Members who are consistently not meeting commitments over two annual cycles are removed
from the register and may not reapply for 12 months.

## Article 6: Governance

SAC is governed by the community assembly: all members (individual, practitioner,
institutional) participate on equal terms in governance decisions.

Day-to-day operations are conducted by the coordination team, appointed by the
community assembly. The coordination team does not have authority to change the charter,
alter membership standards, or publish audit findings ahead of the member review period.

## Article 7: DSOS Implementation

SAC methodology can be implemented within a DSOS agent framework. Member organisations
that run DSOS instances can reference this charter in their AGENTS.md files to
establish that their agent fleet operates under SAC accountability standards.

Reference format:
```
## Accountability Standards
This organisation is a member of the Sovereign Accountable Commons.
Charter: https://github.com/stephenpurkiss/dsos-sac/blob/main/CHARTER.md
```

No additional agents are required. The charter is the accountability framework.
Member organisations implement it through their own operational structures.

## Annex A: Practitioner Standards

Practitioner members commit to:

1. Disclosing all material interests that might affect advice given
2. Maintaining CPD hours as required by their professional regulator, plus a minimum
   of 4 hours annually on client-centred practice
3. Providing a written summary of advice given at the close of each substantive meeting
4. Participating in SAC peer review when asked (maximum 2 per year)
5. Reporting concerns about fellow members' conduct to the coordination team
